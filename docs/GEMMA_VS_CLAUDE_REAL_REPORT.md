# Gemma vs Claude — real-data report comparison (Sapelo2 / Genome_72)

Both reports were generated by the **same** MetaMAVS pipeline on the **same real
GOTTCHA2 results** — sample `Genome_72`, site `sapelo2`, collected `2025-12-02`,
overall risk **High**. Only the LLM backend differed (`LLM_BACKEND`). The
deterministic risk assessment is identical in both; the difference is entirely in
the AI-written **narrative** and per-taxon reasoning.

- Gemma report: `reports/sapelo2_genome72/report.md` (`_Generated by gemma-4-E2B-it_`, 2026-07-10)
- Claude report: `reports/sapelo2_genome72/report_claude_0609.md` (`_Generated by claude-opus-4-8_`, 2026-06-09)

## Headline

| | Gemma 4 E2B | Claude Opus 4.8 |
|---|---|---|
| Report length | 1119 words | 1218 words |
| Overall verdict | High | High (**identical**) |

**Same real data, same verdict.** The gap is depth and epidemiological nuance,
not correctness.

## Where Claude is stronger

1. **Data grounding.** Claude's summary states the real context — *single sample,
   sapelo2, 2025-12-02, GOTTCHA2*, plus "two High, no Critical, no novel
   candidates". Gemma stays generic ("sapelo2 wastewater sample"), omitting the
   date and the single-sample fact.

2. **Single-time-point honesty (the most important difference).** Claude repeats,
   in the summary, per taxon, and the caveats, that the `stable` label comes from
   one time point and **cannot establish a real trend** ("uninformative"). Gemma
   accepts `stable` at face value and even recommends "establish a temporal
   trend" without flagging that the current label is not trustworthy.

3. **Methodological caveats Gemma omits.**
   - "Short reads cannot reliably resolve lineage/subtype" (stated twice).
   - "Single classifier (GOTTCHA2); low-coverage calls can mis-assign; coverage
     breadth/evenness is stronger evidence than read count." — a genuinely
     expert metagenomics point.
   - "8 taxa flagged as potential false positives" — cites the pipeline's own
     false-positive flags.

4. **Recommendation precision.** Claude specifies follow-up ("amplicon/
   targeted-capture sequencing for identity and lineage") and explicitly parks
   Medium detections as routine context needing no immediate action. Gemma gives
   a clean Priority 1/2/3 list but more generic.

## Where Gemma is fine

Gemma is **not** wrong — the verdict is correct and the language is appropriately
cautious: "sequence signals, not confirmed infections", phages reported
separately, orthogonal confirmation recommended, High vs Medium separated. For a
routine, local, free surveillance narrative it is fully adequate.

## Side-by-side excerpts

**Executive summary — Gemma:**
> The metagenomic surveillance of the sapelo2 wastewater sample indicates a High
> overall risk profile … Key signals include the Severe acute respiratory
> syndrome-related coronavirus and the Norwalk virus … These detections represent
> sequence signals, not confirmed infections or outbreaks.

**Executive summary — Claude:**
> This report interprets metagenomic sequence signals from a **single**
> wastewater/environmental sample collected at **sapelo2** on **2025-12-02**,
> classified with GOTTCHA2 … Two taxa were flagged High … both with adequate read
> support but each reported as "stable" from a single time point, **which cannot
> establish a genuine trend**.

**Caveat contrast — Claude only:**
> Single classifier (GOTTCHA2); k-mer/marker calls can mis-assign at low coverage.
> Coverage breadth/evenness, not provided, is stronger evidence than read count.

## Conclusion

Same data, same verdict. Claude wins on single-time-point honesty, methodological
caveats, and data grounding; Gemma is reliable but more general. This matches the
synthetic-data comparison and supports the tiered policy in
[`REAL_DATA_VALIDATION.md`](REAL_DATA_VALIDATION.md): **Gemma for routine local
runs (default), Claude for external or high-risk formal reports.** The
`LLM_BACKEND` switch makes this a zero-code choice.
